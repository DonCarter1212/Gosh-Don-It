Pictured above is Blazblue: Cross Tag Battle (Commonly referred to as BBTAG), a crossover fighting game featuring characters from the Blazblue series (Ragna and Jin, Bottom), Under Night In-Birth series (Hyde and Linne, Left), Persona 4 Arena (Yu and Yosuke, Right), and popular web series RWBY (Ruby and Weiss, Top). I'll save the rant about the awesome concept of a crossover for a later date; this isn't meant to be a blog.

I am an avid gamer with an affinity for competitive games, especially fighters. Currently, I'm playing an anime fighter (most easily identified by their use of sprites rather than three-dimensional models) known as Blazblue: Cross Tag Battle and in contemplation of whether to fully go into the subgenre with Blazblue: Central Fiction or Under Night In-Birth Exe: Late [cl-r] (commonly referred to as BBCF and UNICLR respectively). I will say yes, these names are strange and even confusing, especially UNICLR, which sounds like a rom hack.

I must also comment that fighting game terminology is weird. Out of all the terms I read so far, my personal favorite is "meaty", an adjective to describe an attack that hits someone the moment they stand up. There's a term for such an occurrence because most fighters give you invulnerability as you get back on your feet. An attack is meaty when it hits you the moment that invulnerability wears off.
